Philosophy

I discovered my love for programming in my Junior year of high school, making video games on my calculator during classes. It quickly became something I did during any spare time I had. This is, without a doubt, my passion and what is passion if it cannot be shared. In order to share a passion like this, we programmers have to make sure we are doing things correct, especially in the beginning. It’s more important to me to do something flawlessly the first time than to do what’ll work for now. I feel its vastly more effective and efficient to take the little extra time now, than dealing with a headache later. I also feel the user’s experience is, without a doubt, paramount for a web application to thrive. Getting it right the first time will ensure the satisfaction of the user. I also push myself until the job is finished. While I fully understand the impotance of taking breif breaks while programming, I honestly don't feel I can relax comfortably until I am comfortable with my code.
